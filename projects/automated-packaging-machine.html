Prototype: Automated Fruit Foam Sleeve Packaging Machine

The Problem

To protect fruit from pests and weather before harvesting, farmers often wrap them in foam sleeves. However, the process of preparing these sleeves for easy application is entirely manual. A farmer might have to repeat the same tedious packaging task thousands of times to prepare a sufficient quantity. Witnessing this strenuous and time-consuming labor, I was inspired to develop a machine to automate the process as part of our startup's innovation efforts.

Project Execution & Scope

This project was a proof-of-concept model developed in collaboration with a friend. It's important to note that this prototype focused on a core part of the automation process—placing the foam sleeve into a bag—and did not yet cover the entire end-to-end packaging workflow.

Development Process:

  1. Ideation & Sketching: We started by brainstorming and sketching out the core mechanical concepts for the model.
  2. Prototyping & Assembly: We built the physical model using simple, accessible materials to validate the design.
  3. Embedded Control: I programmed a microcontroller to manage the machine's sequence of operations (using chip ATmega88PA-PU)
  4. PCB Design & Optimization: A custom PCB was designed to create a more integrated and reliable control circuit for the model.
  5. Testing & Future Planning: We demonstrated the model's functionality and researched to develop a industrial-grade version.

Results & Potential

Key Outcome: The prototype successfully performed its intended function, demonstrating that the core automation concept was viable.

Limitations: As a model built with non-industrial components (cardboard structure, basic sensors, and a standard microcontroller), its operational speed was slow.

Potential: Despite its limitations, the project proved that the design has strong potential for real-world application. With further development and industrial-grade mechanical components, it could be scaled into a practical, save time and effort for farmers.
